1. Assuming that a path has no colliders on it, what is the difference between a path being Open and Closed?

A path is open when the variation in one of the variables explains part of the variation in another one. This must be the case in every connected var on the path.

It is closed when at least one variable’s variation remains constant (meaning there is no variation) and no longer has an effect on other variable.

3. Describe the four major differences between randomized experiments and natural experiments discussed in the chapter. As a bonus, there’s a fifth difference described in the chapter having to do with sample size and representativeness.

  1. In RCTs the researcher randomly assigns the treatment to people, whereas in natural experiment this is not the case. Randomization just happened in nature. This might left some back doors open that we need to control for.

  2. In natural experiment people don’t realize they’re in an experiment and sample sizes tend to be bigger.

  3. Natural experiments excludes the variation in treatment that did not occur thanks to the natural randomness. This variation might be important to fully understand the causal relationship.

  4. We are never completely sure that the exogenous source of variation on the treatment is free of back doors.

  5. Sample sizes are smaller in experiments but in RCT’s they are representative of the population from the sampling frame. In a natural experiment the representativeness varies depending on the subpopulation subject to the randomization of treatment. For example,in the Charter school examples, the children that enter into the lottery not necessarily represent the broad student population.

5. Define the concept of exogenous variation.

There is a source of exogenous variation in treatment when there are no open back doors. This means that some of the treatment’s variation is only caused by a random source.
